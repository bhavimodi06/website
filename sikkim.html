Sikkim – Mystic Mountains

Cultural Heritage

Sikkim is a serene and spiritual state nestled in the Himalayas. It has a rich blend of cultures influenced by Buddhism, Nepalese, and Tibetan traditions. The Rumtek Monastery and Namgyal Institute of Tibetology reflect its spiritual core.

Famous Festivals

Losar (Tibetan New Year), Saga Dawa, and Pang Lhabsol are major festivals celebrated with traditional dances, prayer flags, and rituals. These festivals honor Buddhist teachings and the guardian deities of Sikkim.

Cuisine

Sikkimese cuisine is warm, spicy, and hearty. Don’t miss out on momos (dumplings), thukpa (noodle soup), and gundruk soup. Fermented foods and organic vegetables are a staple here.
